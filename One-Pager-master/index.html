Other Services

  • MCNY Purpose Handbooks
  • Interlibrary Loan

  • Interlibrary Loan is used as a way of expanding library resources by obtaining material from other libraries. If you are a student or member of the faculty or staff at MCNY and you need something that is not owned by our library, you may be able to obtain it from another using this service. Books, electronic articles not available through our dababases, and photocopies of journal articles are examples of material available through Interlibrary Loan.
    On average, it takes at least 7-10 days to fill an ILL book request, and 1-2 days for journal articles. Loan periods and renewal options are determined by the lending library.
    If you are interested in requesting an item via Interlibrary loan contact Kate Adler, Reference Librarian at via email: kadler@mcny.edu

  • Library Instruction Sessions

    The Library provides instructional sessions that teach students about the Library's resources and services. These sessions aim to enhance the academic experience of MCNY students, to improve their research skills, and to foster information literacy and critical thinking. The Library encourages faculty in all departments to schedule library instruction sessions for their Constructive Action courses. Together we can introduce your students to: doing research at the college level effectively searching, locating, and evaluating information popular and scholarly resources relevant to a specific assignment basic and advanced searching of library resources, such as the catalog and article databases discipline-specific resources and scholarly communication within that discipline To schedule an library instruction session please contact us at: library@mcny.edu We look forward to working with you and your students.
